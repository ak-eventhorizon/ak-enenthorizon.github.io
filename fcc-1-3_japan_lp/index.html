Katana

Katana is a Japanese sword characterized by a curved, single-edged blade with a circular or squared guard and long grip to accommodate two hands. It was used by the samurai of ancient and feudal Japan.

Tanto

Tanto is a sword, but is used as a knife. The blade is single or double edged with a length between 15 and 30cm. The tanto was designed primarily as a stabbing weapon, but the edge can be used for slashing as well.

Ninjato

Ninjato are allegedly the preferred weapon that the shinobi of feudal Japan carried. It is portrayed by modern ninjutsu practitioners as the weapon of the ninja, and is prominently featured in popular culture.

Odachi

Large/great sword was a type of traditionally made Japanese sword used by the samurai class of feudal Japan. The sword would have a blade length of around 90 centimetres or more. Usually this is infantry sword.

nagamaki

Nagamaki is a long sword with a blade that could be 2 feet or more and a handle of about equal length to the blade. Name "nagamaki" ("long wrapping") is given by the tradition of handle wrapping.

tachi

Tachi and katana generally differ in length, degree of curvature, and how they were worn when sheathed. The tachi koshirae had two hangers which allowed the sword to be worn in a horizontal position with the cutting edge down.
